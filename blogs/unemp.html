8 Months of Growth

A Story about my Unemployment

To anyone of interest about my unemployment,

Job hunting is hard, really really hard. When I’m asked “Did you find a job yet?” or worse, “How come you don’t have a job yet?”, especially if it’s from interviewers, my heart just sinks. But I get those people, I graduated in computer science, so many of my peers received full time offers before they even graduated and the rest, just a couple months later. I was expected to have a job by then. But I guess, sometimes my friends are just trying to catch up with me and the easiest way to ask about my life is to ask about my job.

Throughout my eight months of unemployment, I grew much more than I could have ever imagined. The growth however, wasn’t really what I expected. Actually, in many aspect, I didn’t grow at all. I didn’t learn more than I did in college, I moved back home and I didn’t have to worry about things like in college. I didn’t worry about rent, grocery shopping, or cooking. If you looked at me from the outside, you will see no visible change. But, what got me through all the depression? All the struggle with self-worth? It was God.

Right after I graduated college, my faith was good. I trusted in Jesus and I felt like there was no question that God was real. Jesus died for me and there’s no debate. But the struggles of the last eight months showed me how real God is and how He demands our attention and trust.

Before college, I had a lot of companies that wanted to interview me. I felt blessed, I felt like God gave me so much just because I was so faithful and I earned it. I earned those interviews not because I was a good programmer but because God gave it to me because I love Him. Many months and interviews later, nothing happened but I was actually fine. Soon after, I changed my prayers to not only be thankful but to always align my plans with His plans. Constantly worried each and everyday, I found peace with knowing that when God wants me to have a job, I’ll have one. You might think that this is not a bad mindset, but it wasn’t enough. I didn’t know what it meant to struggle yet. Sure, I trusted God with my future, but it fell quick and my actions reflected that. To me, God wasn’t first on my heart, my career was. In between praying for God’s will, I would be thinking,

“I just had 3 interviews, anytime now God. Which is the one you are going to give to me”

Not only that but then my pride would kick in, I would already be looking at salaries, benefits, housing, and more on salaries. I would tell people I received an offer when no paperworks was signed yet. I would avoid people who would ask about my situations. I would just stay home all day and avoid social media because in reality, I felt so lost and so I just wanted to be alone. At this point, I did only the things I could do, keep applying and praying by myself.

On November 20th, I went to Blue Sky Church in Bellevue. The church was doing a series on growth and that day, they were going over growth through pain. Pastor Steven went over

Romans 5:1-5 Therefore, since we have been justified through faith, we have peace with God through our Lord Jesus Christ, 2 through whom we have gained access by faith into this grace in which we now stand. And we boast in the hope of the glory of God. 3 Not only so, but we also glory in our sufferings, because we know that suffering produces perseverance; 4 perseverance, character; and character, hope. 5 And hope does not put us to shame, because God’s love has been poured out into our hearts through the Holy Spirit, who has been given to us.

A few days ago, I was telling Hyoeun about this passage because she was suffering with the passing of her close friend. The funny thing is that I didn’t think this passage applied to me. I didn’t know I needed this passage. As I sit through church, It hit me like a truck. I needed to realize that I already have peace with God because of Jesus. I should rejoice in my suffering, I need to be happy I’m going through my hardships because in the end, I’m going to get through it because I have Jesus. In my suffering and trials, I’m going to have perseverance, I’m going to have character, and eventually, hope. So at this point, I was a wreck at church, basically breaking down and crying like I haven’t in a long long time. In the midst of emotion, I prayed..



“God, thanks for the last eight months of trial and…

Give me more trials.

Give me more pains.

Let me be unemployed for as long as you want.

As long as I can get close to You, I’ll be happy and fine.”



God works in amazing ways, my unemployment is just one story that God has worked in my life in the past. I know that there’s going to be so many more ways that God can show me who He really is and how important He needs to be in my life. Unemployment was hard, one of the hardest times in my life, but really, I needed that eight months of trials so that I can grow. Just like how God planned it.

Steven
